Title: Expanded Distance based Clustering Protocol (DBCP) in Wireless Sensor Network

Abstract: The main concern of the Wireless sensor network(WSN) are to improve the network lifetime and to utilize the energy of each node effectively. To increase the lifetime as well as stable region of network communication routing protocols play a very important role. The design goal of this protocol is to avoid the data transmissions and reception unnecessarily. In this paper Expanded Distance based Clustering Protocol (DBCP) for wireless sensor network which is an expansion of the Distance based cluster protocol(DBCP) is being introduced. The new protocol considers many parameters and more data sets to evaluate the performance of DBCP. Additionally it compares with more numbers of existing systems to make sure that it outperformed other existing systems. It is based on the method of taking membership during cluster formation to minimize energy dissipation among the sensor networks .This method considers a concept of independent node who is never in other protocols. These independent nodes when nearer to the sink node can directly transmit their own sensor data after compressing them to the sink rather than depends on other nodes. Finally the simulation result depicts that our Expanded DBCP outperformed as compared to some other existing system.
